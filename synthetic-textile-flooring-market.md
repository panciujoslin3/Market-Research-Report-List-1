<p><p>Synthetic textile flooring encompasses three primary types: tufting, woven, and needlefelt. Tufting involves stitching yarn into a backing material, creating various pile heights and textures, ideal for residential and commercial spaces. Woven flooring, created on looms, offers durability and intricate designs, often used in high-end applications. Needlefelt, produced by entangling fibers through a needle-punching process, results in a robust, low-maintenance option suitable for heavy traffic areas. Each type serves distinct aesthetics and performance needs in the synthetic flooring market.</p></p>
