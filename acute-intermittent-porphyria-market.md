<p><p>Acute Intermittent Porphyria (AIP) treatment options include Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone (GnRH) Analogues and prophylactic hematin infusions. GnRH analogues are employed to mitigate symptoms by modulating hormone levels, thereby reducing the frequency of acute attacks. Prophylactic hematin infusions, on the other hand, provide a direct therapeutic approach by replenishing heme levels, essential for normal metabolic functions. Both treatment types aim to manage the condition effectively, minimizing the severity and occurrence of porphyric crises in patients.</p></p>
