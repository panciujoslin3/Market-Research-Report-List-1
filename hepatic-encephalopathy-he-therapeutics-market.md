<p><p>The Hepatic Encephalopathy (HE) therapeutics market comprises various treatment options aimed at managing this neurological condition resulting from liver dysfunction. Lactulose is a primary choice, acting as a laxative to reduce ammonia levels. Rifaximin, an antibiotic, helps decrease gut bacteria that produce ammonia. Neomycin, another antibiotic, also targets ammonia-producing bacteria but is less commonly used due to side effects. Probiotics are emerging for their potential to restore gut flora balance, while Thiamine (Vitamin B1) is important for preventing neurological complications in liver disease.</p></p>
