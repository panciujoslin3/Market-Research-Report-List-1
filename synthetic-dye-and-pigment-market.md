<p><p>The synthetic dye and pigment market comprises various types based on their chemical properties and applications. Acidic dyes, often used for protein fibers, offer vibrant colors but require acidic conditions for fixation. Basic dyes, suitable for acrylic and other synthetic fibers, show high color intensity. Direct dyes are applied directly to fabrics, while reactive dyes form covalent bonds with fibers, ensuring durability. Solvent dyes are soluble in organic solvents and ideal for non-water-based applications, while disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers. Other categories may include specialized or niche dyes and pigments.</p></p>
