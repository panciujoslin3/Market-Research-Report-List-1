<p><ul><li>Cartesian</li><li>SCARA</li><li>6-Axis</li><li>Cylindrical</li><li>Delta</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cooking robot market is segmented into several types based on their arm configurations and functionalities. Cartesian robots utilize linear movements along three axes, ideal for precise stacking or cutting tasks. SCARA robots are designed for high-speed, horizontal movements, suitable for assembly and packaging. 6-axis robots offer versatility and complex maneuvers, making them adept for various cooking techniques. Cylindrical robots work within a cylindrical space, useful for mixing and blending. Delta robots excel at rapid, lightweight tasks like sorting ingredients, while "others" include specialized designs catering to niche applications.</p></p>
